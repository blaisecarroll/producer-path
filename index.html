Common Questions

I am not a natural salesperson. Will this work for me?

Most agents who run events say the same thing, and that is actually an advantage. When you are at a community fair talking with a family about their neighborhood, you are a resource, not a pitch. The system gives you a simple conversation framework that feels like helping, not selling. You collect contact info at the event and follow up when people are ready. Agents who describe themselves as introverts consistently report community events as their most comfortable lead channel.

How much do booths cost, and how much time does this take?

Booth fees at community events typically run from $50 to $400, depending on the event size and your market. Most agents start with one half-day event per month, roughly four to six hours including setup. The calendar we send you includes estimated booth costs for every event near you, so you can pick the ones that fit your budget and schedule before committing to anything.

Do people actually buy insurance at community events?

Not at the event itself, and that is the point. You are not writing policies at the booth. You are meeting the people who will become clients over the next 60 to 90 days. The families at a county fair who stop to talk to you already live in your market, own homes and cars, and need insurance. They just are not ready to buy at that moment. You follow up afterward. Because they remember meeting you in person, they are far more likely to take your call than a stranger who has never heard your name.
